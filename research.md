## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **PMI PMBOK (Project Management Body of Knowledge)** — global standard for project management methodology; engineering firms often require software to support PMI-aligned processes and terminology
- **Earned Value Management (ANSI/EIA-748)** — government-mandated standard for tracking cost and schedule performance on federally funded engineering projects; software must calculate BCWS, BCWP, and ACWP
- **FAR / DCAA Compliance** — Federal Acquisition Regulations and Defense Contract Audit Agency requirements for firms with US government contracts, including compliant indirect cost rate structures
- **ISO 9001:2015** — quality management system standard widely adopted by engineering consultancies; practice management systems must support document control and process traceability
- **ISO 19650** — international BIM standard for information management in the built environment; relevant for civil and structural engineering deliverable coordination
- **ASCE Standards** — American Society of Civil Engineers technical and professional standards referenced in deliverable quality management workflows
- **FIDIC Contract Forms** — internationally recognised engineering contract templates that define milestone billing and deliverable acceptance processes

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global project management software market is valued at USD 9.6–11.3 billion in 2026, growing at 13–15% CAGR to potentially reach USD 23–39 billion by 2031–2035. The AEC-specific segment represents a meaningful slice, dominated by Deltek's installed base of 12,000+ A/E firms.

**Funding:** Deltek (Thoma Bravo portfolio) is the dominant enterprise player. BigTime Software has received growth capital. Birdview and Productive continue venture-funded expansion. Microsoft Project remains a complementary tool funded internally by Microsoft's broader enterprise cloud strategy.

**Pricing Landscape:** Enterprise platforms (Deltek) are quoted in the $50,000+ annual range for mid-size firms. Mid-market tools (BQE CORE, BigTime) run $20–$50/user/month. Lighter tools start at $10–$20/user/month. Government-contract-compliant deployments carry significant implementation overhead for DCAA audit readiness.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Small civil or structural engineering consultancies (need simple time/billing), mid-size multi-discipline engineering firms (need portfolio resource planning and phase billing), large government-contracting engineering firms (need DCAA-compliant accounting), infrastructure project management offices (need schedule, earned value, and risk management integration).

**Notable Trends:** Market is shifting from standalone task tracking to integrated work management platforms combining financial management, resource planning, and analytics. AI-assisted scheduling and risk prediction are emerging differentiators. Earned value tracking and phase-based billing are moving from large-firm niche to mid-market expectation as clients demand greater project transparency.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **AI-driven earned value forecasting** that predicts cost-at-completion and schedule-at-completion from early project data patterns, surfacing likely overruns weeks before they appear in traditional reporting
- **Automated subconsultant and subcontract management** that tracks deliverable milestones, payment triggers, and insurance certificate expiry across the supply chain without manual spreadsheet maintenance
- **Intelligent resource allocation** that matches engineer skill profiles and availability against upcoming project needs across the portfolio, reducing bench time and preventing over-commitment
- **Natural language project status reporting** that synthesises schedule, budget, and risk data from the project database and drafts client-facing milestone reports and internal management summaries
- **AI-assisted scope change detection** that monitors project correspondence, drawing revisions, and RFI logs for patterns indicating scope creep and generates change order documentation for project manager review
